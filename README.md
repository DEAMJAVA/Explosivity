# Explosivity

Ever wanted Creepers to stop blowing up your builds, while still being dangerous? Or maybe you want TNT to level mountains without instantly killing nearby players?

**Explosivity** gives you fine-grained control over Minecraft's explosions through simple gamerules. Every explosion type can have its **entity damage** and **block damage** adjusted independently, letting you tailor the experience to your world or server.

Whether you're running an SMP, building an adventure map, or creating a chaotic PvP experience, Explosivity makes explosion balancing simple.

## Features

### 💥 Customize Every Major Explosion

Configure separate damage multipliers for:

* TNT
* TNT Minecarts
* Creepers
* Charged Creepers
* End Crystals
* Beds
* Respawn Anchors

Each explosion type has **two independent multipliers**:

* **Entity Damage** – How much damage it deals to players and mobs.
* **Block Damage** – How much damage it deals to the world.

## Endless Possibilities

With Explosivity, you can easily create experiences like:

* TNT that destroys terrain without harming players.
* Creepers that are deadly but leave your builds untouched.
* Powerful End Crystals for PvP while keeping world damage under control.
* Ridiculously powerful bed explosions.
* A completely peaceful world where explosions don't damage anything.

## Gamerules

Each setting is available as a standard Minecraft gamerule.

Every multiplier defaults to **1.0**, matching vanilla Minecraft.

* **0.0** → Disable damage completely
* **1.0** → Vanilla behavior
* **Above 1.0** → More powerful explosions
* **Between 0.0 and 1.0** → Reduced damage

## Why Explosivity?

Minecraft's built-in gamerules only let you toggle certain explosion behaviors on or off. Explosivity gives you much more control by letting you decide **how powerful** each explosion should be, instead of forcing an all-or-nothing choice.
